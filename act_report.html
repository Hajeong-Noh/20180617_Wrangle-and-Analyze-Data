1. Does the tweet with higher rating get more retweets or favorites?¶

rating_outlier_limit = (df.rating_numerator / df.rating_denominator).quantile(0.99)
retweet_outlier_limit = df.retweet_count.quantile(0.99)

df_filter1 = df[((df.rating_numerator / df.rating_denominator) < rating_outlier_limit) & (df.retweet_count < retweet_outlier_limit)]

Rating and retweet count show a positive relationship, which means if a user uploads a WeRateDogs tweet with a good rating, it is more likely to be retweeted by other users. If you want your tweet more retweeted by other users, be generous on rating a dog :)

2. What time do the people upload WeRateDogs tweets?¶

In [16]:

df.timestamp = pd.to_datetime(df.timestamp)

# Histogram of tweet upload time
sns.distplot(df.timestamp.dt.hour, bins=24, kde=False);
plt.xlim(0,23);
plt.xticks(range(0, 24, 1));
plt.xlabel('Tweet Upload Time')
plt.ylabel('Observation rate');
plt.title('Distribution of tweet upload time');

A very interesting graph is drawn. The histogram of tweet upload time shows a bimodal distribution. Users upload the WeRateDogs tweet between midnight to 2 AM the most and quite a lot between 3 PM and 4 PM as well. The tweets are rarely observed in the morning and early afternoon. People might love to share their life on the bed just before getting asleep :)

3. Does the tweet with a picture type get a higher rating?¶

# Change the missing values in picture type column to analaysis
df_for_figtype_analysis = df
df_for_figtype_analysis.picture_type = df_for_figtype_analysis.picture_type.fillna('notype')

# Average rating by type
avg_rating_by_type = df_for_figtype_analysis.groupby(df_for_figtype_analysis.picture_type).mean()[['rating_numerator', 'rating_denominator']]
avg_rating_by_type = avg_rating_by_type.rating_numerator / avg_rating_by_type.rating_denominator

# Bar graph of averge rating by picture type
sns.barplot(x=avg_rating_by_type.index, y=avg_rating_by_type, order=['doggo', 'floofer', 'pupper', 'puppo', 'notype'],
           palette=['red', 'orange', 'yellow', 'green', 'gray']);
plt.xlabel('Picture types')
plt.ylabel('Rating (rating numerator / rating denominator)');
plt.title('Rating by picture types');

The tweets without picture type and with picture type do not show a significant difference in rating. In terms of the tweets with picture types (not null), even though 'pupper' picture type has a relatively row average rating comparing to other types, it does not mean it has a practical meaning when we consider that the 'pupper' and 'puppo' have actually very similar meaning.
